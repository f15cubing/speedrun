# Critique 13 — Stress-inoculation & interventions: comparative, evidence-ranked

**Verdict: strong, accept — with one important reconciliation against prompt 14.** Excellent comparative discipline: every row separates [objective-score] from [anxiety], names real-exam-vs-lab transfer, and rates app-deliverability. Closes SQ13.

## What is genuinely useful (carry forward)
- **The ranking is the deliverable** [strong]: (1) arousal reappraisal, (2) realistic timed simulated testing / graded exposure, (3) sustained (~2-week) mindfulness, (4) relaxation/breathing, (5) expressive writing **demoted**, (6) full SIT (not app-faithful — harvest *components*), (7) CBM (insufficient).
- **Expressive writing demoted** [strong]: SSRP failed replication (Camerer et al. 2018); the original "d≈2.48" is itself an implausible-magnitude red flag.
- **Brief mindfulness is null; only the sustained 2-week course transfers** (Mrazek 2013, ~16-percentile GRE-reading boost). Don't market a one-off 10-min meditation as a score booster. **[strong]**
- **SIT is not faithfully self-administrable** — harvest its components (psychoeducation + reappraisal + breathing + graded practice) rather than the multi-session clinician protocol. **[strong]**
- **The sharp meta-observation** [strong]: *brevity/app-friendliness correlates inversely with replication robustness* — the most scalable interventions (brief reappraisal, expressive writing, brief mindfulness) are precisely the ones exposed to publication-bias and failed-replication concerns. This is the right lens for a hackathon "ship the cheap thing" temptation.
- **Mechanisms** [moderate-strong]: WM/attentional protection (ACT, Eysenck 2007) is the directly score-relevant channel for WM-intensive GRE-Math; choking (Beilock & Carr) and challenge-vs-threat (Blascovich/Mendes, publication-bias-flagged) explain *why*, not *that*.

## Problems / the key reconciliation
1. **13's "reappraisal is the single best pick" is over-stated relative to the newest evidence.** It calls reappraisal "replicated on community-college math exams (Jamieson 2016, 2022)" but those are **Jamieson's own labs**; it does **not** cite the **2026 multi-institution Thormodsæter et al. replication that came back null on both anxiety and performance, with no moderation** (surfaced in prompt 14). **Reconcile:** reappraisal replicates *within-lab*, fails in *independent multi-site* replication → treat as fragile; plan for d ≈ 0–0.2, not the d≈1.0 GRE single-study figure. 14 supersedes 13 on this verdict. **[important]**
2. **Graded timed exposure's isolated score effect is under-quantified** — honestly flagged. Its real strength is that it *doubles as content practice* and is justified by the **speededness construct** (SQ8), so it carries the lowest replication risk. Carry that reframing (it's the spine of prompt 14's recommendation).
3. **Bosshard & Gomez 2024 (k=44, d=0.23)** is the right anchor for realistic magnitude — keep that, not the original GRE d. Flag the citation as recent/verify.

## Decision
- **No follow-up.** SQ13 closed.
