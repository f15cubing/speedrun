# Critique 15 — Testing-UI: design, ablation, interface decision

**Verdict: strong, accept — closes SQ15 and resolves the testing-UI interface question.** The deliverable is disciplined in exactly the way this corpus rewards: it keeps **[objective-score/latency]** strictly separate from **[self-report/usability-scale]**, refuses to fabricate a UI "effect," and lands the same *shape* of verdict the anxiety thread (SQ14) reached — **COMPLEMENT, do not replace, and do not let it consume the single ablation slot.** It reuses the SQ11 leakage-isolated bank and the SQ8 speededness construct rather than inventing new machinery. Closes SQ15.

## What is genuinely useful (carry forward)
- **The decision [strong]: COMPLEMENT via an opt-in "Exam Mode" that gates the *Readiness* score only**, with the validated minimal-chrome baseline retained as the default daily loop (and left untouched for Memory/Performance measurement). This is the correct call: it captures the one defensible win (ecological validity of Readiness) without a permanent second co-equal UI.
- **The one strong, non-confounded argument [strong]:** a faithful exam shell improves the **ecological validity of the Readiness score**, which is explicitly benchmarked to the real speeded, rights-only, computer-delivered exam. The **answer-review evidence is the sharpest instance** — Bridgeman (2012) + Liu et al. (2015, large-sample GRE) show the real exam's review/change affordance materially and positively affects scores, so a Readiness estimate produced *without* review is biased. This is a measurement-validity point, not a "UI boosts learning" claim, and it survives scrutiny.
- **Measurement plan [strong]:** primary outcome = objective **score + per-item latency** on a timed held-out partition (P2), under ~2.6 min/item; secondary = **SUS** (benchmarked to Sauro & Lewis mean 68), **NASA-TLX**, task-time, misclick rate. The **anti-contamination safeguards** (self-report collected only *after* the objective post-test, neutral framing, self-report cannot override a null primary) are exactly the same discipline SQ12/SQ14 demanded, applied to demand-toward-liking-the-novel-UI.
- **Honest-null machinery [strong]:** delayed (≥1 wk) under-pressure endpoint, within-subject crossover, **TOST in two units (±2 items AND ±15 s/item)**, explicit "underpowered → estimation/feasibility, not a significance test." Consistent with SQ4/SQ11/SQ14.
- **Falsifiable flip [strong]:** elevate toward REPLACE only if the **first-time digital test-taker** subgroup shows a delayed under-pressure score advantage exceeding the +2-item SESOI with CI excluding zero. Names the subgroup and the result that flips the call — precisely the contingent framing the prompt required.
- **Risk table [strong]:** measurement contamination via *interface familiarity* (the central threat — a score gain that is learning-irrelevant), cognitive-load harm to low performers from navigator/flag chrome (Chandler & Sweller), novelty decay (Rodrigues et al. 2022), opportunity cost vs content/interleaving (Pan & Rickard d=0.40), accessibility regressions, tiny-n over-claiming — each with a mitigation.

## Problems / verification flags
1. **Cross-thread power/slot reconciliation is under-stated — MUST be made explicit in synthesis. [important, not a defect in the science]** The project has exactly **one** pre-registered ablation slot, held by **interleaving** (SQ4), and SQ14 already ruled that anxiety inoculation must *not* take it. A UI ablation is now a **third** claimant on the same tiny-n power budget. The deliverable's own logic (COMPLEMENT, opt-in, "the pilot uses ONE treatment") is consistent with this, but it should say plainly: **the UI is NOT the ablated learning-science feature.** Its evaluation is a **secondary interface-validity estimation pilot** that should *piggyback on the periodic mock/exam-pressure-simulator sessions already justified by speededness (SQ8/SQ14)* — not a second pre-registered ablation competing with interleaving. Three ablated features at tiny n = three uninterpretable nulls.
2. **On-screen countdown-timer evidence is genuinely thin — do not oversell the timer. [verify / low-confidence]** The only direct timer studies cited are He (2024, n=21 undergraduates, VDT visual search) and Hallez & Vallier (2025, n=44 *children*). There is **no direct experiment on a persistent countdown clock in adult high-stakes CBT.** The response says this honestly ("a genuine gap"), and the timer is justified on *fidelity* grounds rather than a performance claim — which is the right move — but tag the timer-chrome claims **[verify]** and keep them fidelity-only.
3. **The van der Linden, Jeon & Ferrara (2011) "erratum/retraction" is load-bearing for the answer-review verdict — verify before quoting. [verify]** The claim that they argued review *harms* scores and then withdrew the empirical basis via erratum (model non-convergence) is used to neutralize the one contrary source. Confirm the erratum wording. Even if it is softer than stated, Bridgeman 2012 + Liu et al. 2015 carry the "review helps / should be allowed" verdict independently, so the recommendation is robust.
4. **A few recent/secondary cites need a build-time check [verify]:** He (2024) and Hallez & Vallier (2025) journal/volume details; the PMC10299616 "d = 0.35" speededness figure; the Rodrigues et al. (2022) novelty-window figures; Van der Kleij et al. (2015) EF/KR/KCR effect sizes (0.49 / 0.05 / 0.32). None are decision-critical; the COMPLEMENT call stands without any single one.
5. **±2-item / ±15 s-per-item SESOI are reasonable placeholders, not derived.** As with SQ14's ±2-item bound, set these from the *actual* SD of the timed held-out set once data exist; ±15 s/item in particular should be re-derived from observed latency dispersion.
6. **Minor:** the "flow vs dashboard" (component c) and "mobile vs desktop" (component d) rows are practice-mode affordances / covariates, not part of the exam-shell contrast being ablated. Keep them out of the primary UI arm so the exam-shell vs baseline contrast stays clean; device is a **stratifier/covariate**, not a manipulated arm.

## Decision
- **No follow-up.** SQ15 closed. This resolves the testing-UI interface question and does **not** dislodge interleaving from the ablation slot.
- **Carry to synthesis [strong]:** **Baseline minimal-chrome review UI stays the default** for Memory/Performance. Ship an **opt-in "Exam Mode" (authentic exam shell) gating Readiness only** — persistent 2h50m clock, item-navigator grid, flag-for-review, no-pause, answer-review permitted (per Bridgeman/Liu), deferred feedback. Justify it primarily on **Readiness ecological validity + answer-review measurement bias**, secondarily on speededness (SQ8) — **not** on a "UI improves scores" claim. It reuses the exam-pressure-simulator sessions from SQ14; it is **not** a second ablation.
- **Retags:** COMPLEMENT-not-replace **[strong]**; opt-in-Exam-Mode-gates-Readiness-only **[strong]**; answer-review-must-be-allowed (Bridgeman 2012 / Liu et al. 2015) **[strong]**; ecological-validity-of-Readiness **[strong]**; objective-score UI effect small/confounded/undetectable-at-tiny-n **[moderate]**; countdown-timer performance evidence **[verify / thin]**; van der Linden erratum **[verify]**; UI-must-not-consume-the-single-ablation-slot **[strong, cross-thread]**.
