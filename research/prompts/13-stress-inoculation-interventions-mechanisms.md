# Sub-prompt 13 — Stress inoculation & test-anxiety interventions: comparative evidence + mechanisms

You are a specialist research agent in the **psychology of stress, anxiety interventions, and performance under pressure**. Assume the foundational question (does test anxiety causally depress scores?) is handled in sub-prompt 12. Your job: **comparatively evaluate the candidate interventions** a GRE-Math study app could deliver, rank them by **evidence-to-implementability**, and pin down the **mechanisms** — with a hard requirement to separate "reduces anxiety" from "improves real timed-exam scores," and "self-administered/brief" from "clinician/multi-session."

## The exact question

**Part 1 — Comparative intervention review.** For EACH intervention below give: core procedure; best effect size (with CI) on **objective performance** *and* separately on anxiety; **transfer evidence to real standardized/timed math exams** (not just lab tasks or self-report); typical dose & who delivers it; and **app-deliverability** (can a phone/desktop app administer it faithfully?).
- **Stress Inoculation Training (SIT)** — Meichenbaum's 3-phase model (conceptualization → skills acquisition & rehearsal → application under graded stressors); the literal "inoculation" idea. Saunders, Driskell, Johnston & Salas (1996) meta-analysis. Is it tested on academic exams? Self-administrable?
- **Arousal reappraisal** — Jamieson, Mendes, Blascovich and esp. **Jamieson et al. (2010), "Turning the knots in your stomach into bows," which used GRE quantitative material / actual GRE**; reframing arousal as helpful. Brief, scalable.
- **Expressive writing / "worry-dump" before the exam** — **Ramirez & Beilock (2011, *Science*), "Writing about testing worries boosts exam performance in the classroom."** Brief, trivially app-deliverable; note replication/heterogeneity status.
- **Systematic desensitization / graded exposure / realistic simulated timed testing** — practice under authentic time pressure and stakes; the closest to "inoculation" an app can build.
- **Relaxation / paced breathing / PMR.**
- **Mindfulness meditation** — **Mrazek, Franklin, Phillips, Baird & Schooler (2013, *Psychological Science*)** (improved GRE reading + working memory); brief vs sustained-practice versions.
- **(optional) Attentional control / cognitive-bias-modification training.**

**Part 2 — Mechanisms.** For each plausible channel, label **[empirical]** vs **[theoretical]**, rate evidence strength, and say whether it plausibly moves a **timed multiple-choice** score:
- Working-memory / attentional protection — **Attentional Control Theory** (Eysenck, Derakshan, Santos, Calvo 2007) and processing-efficiency theory.
- Choking under pressure (Beilock & Carr 2005) and its reduction.
- Challenge-vs-threat appraisal / biopsychosocial model (Blascovich & Mendes).
- Desensitization / habituation of the stress response.

## Deliverable format and length
- ~1300–1900 words.
- Section A: intervention comparison table (intervention / procedure / effect on objective score + CI / effect on anxiety / real-timed-exam transfer? / dose & deliverer / **app-deliverable?** / confidence).
- Section B: mechanisms table (channel / empirical-or-theoretical / strength / moves a timed-MC score?).
- Section C: ranking by **evidence-to-implementability for a self-serve app**, with the top 1–2 picks justified.
- Section D: the honest gaps — where evidence is on self-report only, lab-only, or fails to replicate.

## Sourcing requirements
- Prefer peer-reviewed RCTs and meta-analyses; name researchers (Meichenbaum, Saunders, Jamieson, Mendes, Ramirez, Beilock, Mrazek, Schooler, Eysenck, Derakshan) and note disagreements.
- Per claim: source, type, confidence; **separate [anxiety outcome] from [objective-score outcome]** and flag **replication status** for the famous brief-intervention results (reappraisal, expressive writing, mindfulness) — several have contested or mixed replications; report them honestly.
- Never invent effect sizes.

## Counter-evidence / weak-spot demands
- Flag the **replication crisis exposure** of the most app-friendly findings (brief reappraisal, expressive writing, brief mindfulness) — note any failed/mixed replications and publication-bias concerns.
- Distinguish interventions validated on **real timed exams** (Jamieson, Ramirez & Beilock, Mrazek) from those validated only on lab tasks or self-report.
- Note **moderation**: several effects appear concentrated in **high-anxiety** individuals and may be null/negative for low-anxiety students.
- Be explicit where the strongest-evidence option (e.g., full SIT) is **not** realistically self-administrable in an app.

## Source list (REQUIRED, at the very bottom)
End with a `## Sources` section listing **every** source you explicitly cited **and** any you drew ideas from (author/title/venue/year + link/DOI), grouped by type. No uncited claims.

End with a self-confidence summary and a one-line verdict: which 1–2 interventions have the best evidence-to-implementability for an app, and the biggest caveat.
